My favorite food is the Japanese beef bowl, or 牛丼 (gyuudon). As you may suspect, it's a bowl: a bowl filled with rice and topped with marinated beef and caramelized onions, and usually garnished with pickled red ginger and seasoning. It's the quintessential Japanese fast food; it's fast, delicious, and filling.

Yoshinoya is a great place to get beef bowls day-to-day, but my favorite restaurant to eat it at is Hana Ichimonme at Downtown Los Angeles' Little Tokyo. The restaurant itself is a little like a time machine back to the 80's, but the food is beautifully presented, and delicately delicious.
