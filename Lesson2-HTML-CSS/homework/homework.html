Panelada is a very traditional dish from the state of Ceará, in Brazil. It's made out of pig's intestines and stomach. Those are, then, washed thoroughly and richly seasoned, then cooked with white onions, garlic and bell peppers. Panelada is usually served with white rice, corn couscous and mandioca flour (farinha). Although it's a very traditional dish, it is not rare to find many people from Ceará who find it disgusting. O Mar Menino
