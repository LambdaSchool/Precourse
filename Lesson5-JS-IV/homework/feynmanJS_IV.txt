1. Feynman Writing Prompts - Write out explanations of the following concepts like you are explaining it to a 12 year old.  Doing this will help you quickly discover any holes in your understanding.  Ask your questions on Slack.
		
	* Callback Functions -- A callback function is a function that is passed into another function, and is called inside of the other function.
	* `arguments` - Arguments are the individual argument that goes into a parameter of a function, and goes at the top, in the parentheses.  A good analogy to differentiate the two that I found on Stack Overflow is that the parameter is the (p)arking space, while the argument is the (a)utomobile.
